Proposed Product Roadmap

From Finance Copilot to Decision Intelligence

This is a forward-looking strategy artifact, not a description of every currently implemented capability. The current demo implements the official-source peer fact layer, typed comparison tools, comparability guardrails, and audit evidence; diagnostic scenarios and automated ingestion remain roadmap items.

Executive Summary

  • Add peers, but do not build a generic comparison table. Use each company for a specific benchmark: Uber for platform economics, Lyft for Mobility, DoorDash for Deliveries, and Sea for Southeast Asian ecosystem and fintech economics.
  • The differentiation should be analytical tools, not a larger document corpus. The copilot should diagnose gaps, isolate drivers, run scenarios, measure guidance pacing, and recommend actions using deterministic calculations.
  • Demo one killer workflow. Ask why Grab's margin differs from a relevant peer, quantify the gap, simulate the controllable levers, and return an executive insight card with evidence, action, confidence, and risks.

The power comes from six decision tools

Diagnose

Peer Gap Analyzer

Normalizes GMV/Gross Bookings/GOV, take rate, EBITDA margin, growth, user intensity, and FCF conversion. It explains which gaps are real and which are definition artifacts.

Decompose

Driver Tree Engine

Breaks revenue into volume × monetization and EBITDA into revenue × margin, then attributes change to mix, incentives, credit losses, corporate costs, and operating leverage.

Simulate

Scenario Lab

Answers “what if” using deterministic formulas: GMV growth, take rate, incentives, cost-to-serve, loan growth, credit losses, and corporate-cost scenarios with base, upside, and downside cases.

Anticipate

Guidance & Early-Warning Monitor

Measures actual performance against guidance, flags required exit rates, detects peer divergence, and highlights metrics that make guidance increasingly difficult or easier to achieve.

Interpret

Management Signal Tracker

Compares earnings-call language and guidance changes period over period, links every signal to an exact quote, and separates management claims from measured financial evidence.

Act

Executive Insight Cards

Every answer ends with: observation, quantified driver, business implication, recommended action, confidence level, counter-evidence, and source trail.

The killer interview demo is a closed-loop decision

Executive question: “Grab's Deliveries margin improved, but how does it compare with Uber Delivery and DoorDash, what explains the gap, and which controllable lever closes it fastest without sacrificing growth?”

Copilot response: normalize peer definitions → quantify the margin gap → isolate monetization, incentives, mix, and cost drivers → simulate three actions → recommend one action with expected EBITDA impact, risk, and confidence → show citations and arithmetic.

1. RetrieveVersioned filing facts and metric definitions
2. NormalizeComparable denominators and exclusions
3. AnalyzeDrivers, scenarios, pacing, and risks
4. RecommendAction, impact, confidence, and audit trail

Recommended build sequence

  1. Peer Lens — partially implemented: FY2023-FY2025 common peer facts, a metric-definition registry, typed company/segment/comparison tools, and comparability notes are in the current demo. Deeper peer segment extraction and a dedicated benchmark tab remain.
  2. Insight Engine: add `diagnose_peer_gap`, a volume/rate/mix driver tree, and executive insight cards. This is the highest interview-value step.
  3. Scenario Lab: add deterministic assumptions and base/upside/downside outputs. Keep the LLM outside the calculations.
  4. Production vision: show automated SEC/IR ingestion, XBRL mapping, restatement detection, scheduled refresh, evaluation, and approval workflows as architecture rather than building all of them for the demo.

Do not prioritize yet: stock-price prediction, generic sentiment scores, unconstrained web search, or an autonomous investment recommendation. They add risk and distract from the finance-operations case.

Further Questions

  • Should the primary persona be a CFO/FP&A leader, a business-unit operator, or an investor-relations analyst?
  • Which single decision should the interview demo optimize: margin improvement, guidance attainment, capital allocation, or peer benchmarking?
  • Should quarterly data be added now, or should the first peer version preserve the current annual FY2023-FY2025 scope?

Caveats and Assumptions

Reported non-GAAP definitions are company-specific. The peer layer must store the original definition, normalized definition, denominator, exclusions, period, and source. Where normalization is not defensible, the copilot should say “directionally comparable” or refuse the ranking rather than manufacture precision.
